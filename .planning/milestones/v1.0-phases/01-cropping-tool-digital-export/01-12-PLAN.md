The crop-workspace is a flex-column (align-items: center).
Inside it, crop-assembly is a flex-row (align-items: stretch) containing:
  1. .ruler (2rem width, flex-shrink: 0) — vertical ruler, left
  2. .crop-frame (aspect-ratio: 35/45, max-height: 80vh) — the photo viewport, center
  3. .dims (3.5rem width, flex-shrink: 0) — dimension brackets, right

Below crop-assembly (still inside crop-workspace), .ruler-h sits as a sibling.
It currently has `width: {frameWidth}px` inline style and `margin-left: calc(2rem + 2px)` in CSS.

The problem: margin-left is a hardcoded guess at the vertical ruler width + borders.
If it's even 1px off, the ruler doesn't align with the crop frame.

The fix must make the horizontal ruler's left and right edges structurally match
the crop frame, not depend on matching sibling widths.
